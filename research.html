Research

I have a multidisciplinary background. I love physics, mathematics, computational science and their application to biological systems, principally neuroscience. In the last years I've been learning a lot of neurobiology and developed an interest to understand motivated behavior.

Nowadays

I am currently working in the motivated behavior laboratory (look at the photo on the right to know us), in charge of Dr. Luis Tellez (at the center of the image...clearly), in the Institute of neurobiology UNAM. I am studying the role of basal ganglia circuits in decision making and locomotion, using electrophisology tools like LFP recording.

As undergraduate student

In the Biomedical Physics degree I obtained physical, biological and mathematical bases that have helped me understand and analyze biological processes from a multidisciplinary point of view. I had the opportunity to learn programming mainly in Python and obtained the basic knowledge of Matlab and C language, my taste for programming and neurosciences allowed me to work on a project for the development and validation of a biophysical mathematical model for studying aging in three types of hippocampal neuron to obtain my degree.
